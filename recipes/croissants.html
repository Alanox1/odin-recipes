Croissants

The croissant, a delight of French pastry, emerges as a golden, crescent-shaped laminated dough. With delicately layered flakiness and a subtly buttery taste, each bite offers a crispy exterior and a soft interior, making this pastry a true morning delicacy or a companion during moments of indulgence.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Fresh yeast
  • Milk
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Mix flour, salt, and sugar in a bowl.
  2. Dissolve fresh yeast in warm milk.
  3. Add yeast mixture to the dry ingredients and knead into a dough.
  4. Roll out the dough into a rectangle.
  5. Place cold butter in the center of the dough and fold the sides over it.
  6. Roll out the dough again, then fold it in thirds like a letter.
  7. Repeat the rolling and folding process a few times, chilling the dough between each fold.
  8. Roll out the dough one last time and cut it into triangles.
  9. Roll each triangle into a croissant shape.
  10. Let the croissants rise until puffy.
  11. Bake in a preheated oven until golden and flaky.
  12. Cool on a wire rack before enjoying!
